## 📁 Projects

### 🏦 Bank Customer Churn Analysis

> **"Why are customers leaving — and what can we do about it?"**

**The Business Problem**

A bank was losing customers without a clear understanding of *who* was leaving or *why*. This project digs into customer data to find the warning signs of churn (when a customer closes their account or stops using services) and recommends targeted strategies to improve retention.

**What Was Done**

| Step | Description |
|------|-------------|
| 🧹 Data Cleaning | Started with a messy, real-world spreadsheet and transformed it into a clean, analysis-ready dataset |
| 🔍 Data Analysis | Examined 10,000+ customer records across age, country, credit score, account balance, and activity level |
| 📊 Dashboard | Built an interactive Power BI dashboard so stakeholders can filter and explore the data themselves |
| 📝 Business Report | Wrote a plain-language report summarising key findings and retention recommendations |
| 🎯 Strategy Document | Produced a targeted retention strategy — *Precision Retention* — outlining specific actions for high-risk customer segments |

**Key Findings (Plain Language)**

- Customers in **Germany** churned at roughly twice the rate of those in France or Spain
- **Older customers (ages 45–60)** were significantly more likely to leave, despite having higher balances
- Customers using **only one product** had the highest churn rate — cross-selling is a clear opportunity
- **Inactive members** were far more likely to churn — early re-engagement campaigns could make a measurable difference
